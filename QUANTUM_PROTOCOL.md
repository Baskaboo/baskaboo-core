## 1. Hypotheses Formulation

We consider an electronic quasicrystal system described by a $C^*$-algebra of observables $\mathcal{A}$, represented faithfully as bounded operators on a separable Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$, $\pi: \mathcal{A} \to B(\mathcal{H})$. The Baskaboo framework postulates a non-commutative structure captured by two effective Hamiltonian operators $H_{+\Phi}, H_{-\Phi} \in \mathcal{A}$, associated with dual "passes" of electronic transport, parametrized by a real scalar $\Phi$ linked to the quasicrystal’s structural phase.

Let $K_0(\mathcal{A})$ denote the even K-theory group of $\mathcal{A}$, and let $\nu \in K_0(\mathcal{A})$ be a topological invariant defined via a Fermi projection or spectral projection associated with the relevant energy window of transport. We formulate the following hypotheses:

### $H_1$: Algebraic Non-Commutativity as a Topological Signal
There exists a non-trivial class $\nu \in K_0(\mathcal{A})$ such that:
1. The commutator $[H_{+\Phi}, H_{-\Phi}] \neq 0$ in $B(\mathcal{H})$.
2. The value of a physically measurable transport invariant $T$ (e.g., a quantized conductance plateau, a Chern-type invariant, or an index) is a deterministic function of $\nu$, i.e.,
$$T = f(\nu)$$
with $f$ fixed a priori and independent of microscopic disorder within a specified class of samples.

* **Failure Mode:** If $[H_{+\Phi}, H_{-\Phi}] = 0$ in the experimentally relevant representation, or if $T$ is experimentally found to be independent of $\nu$ within statistical power, then $H_1$ is falsified.

### $H_2$: Bilayer-Specific Double-Pass Phenomenology
Let $S_1, S_2, S_3$ denote, respectively, monolayer, bilayer, and trilayer realizations of the electronic quasicrystal with otherwise matched material parameters (composition, disorder range, temperature, and external fields). The Double-Pass postulate requires:
1. There exists a spectral observable $\sigma(E)$ (e.g., local density of states or transmission spectrum) and a derived signature $\Delta_{\text{DP}}$ (e.g., a characteristic peak splitting, avoided crossing, or directional transport asymmetry) such that:
$$\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_2) \neq 0$$
while
$$\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_1) \approx 0, \quad \Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_3) \approx 0$$
within a pre-defined noise tolerance band.
2. The sign and magnitude of $\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_2)$ are specified a priori by the algebra defined for $H_{+\Phi}, H_{-\Phi}$ and cannot be reproduced by standard bilayer models without the non-commutative structure.

* **Failure Mode:** If the same class of spectral/transport signature appears in $S_1$ or $S_3$, or if $S_2$ fails to exhibit the predicted unique $\Delta_{\text{DP}}$ distinct from conventional bilayer theory, $H_2$ is falsified.

### $H_3$: Phase-Memory Constrained by a 0.382 Gap Fraction
Let the quasicrystal host an effectively "open" fractional gap characterized by a dimensionless parameter $g = 0.00382$ ($0.382\%$). Define a phase-memory metric $R$ as:
$$R = \frac{C_{\text{out}}}{C_{\text{in}}}$$
where $C_{\text{in}}$ and $C_{\text{out}}$ are, respectively, input and output values of a phase-coherence correlator (e.g., interference fringe visibility, off-diagonal density-matrix element, or two-time correlator) across the transport path. 

$H_3$ states that the presence of a gap fraction $g = 0.00382$ imposes a quantitatively constrained, monotone relation:
$$R = F(g)$$
with $F$ fixed a priori, such that deviations $|R_{\text{exp}} - F(0.00382)| > \delta_R$ (for a pre-registered tolerance $\delta_R$) constitute falsification.

### $H_4$: $\Phi$ as an Unforgeable Structural Input
Let $\Phi$ denote a structural or geometric parameter required for the definition of $H_{+\Phi}$ and $H_{-\Phi}$ (e.g., a golden-ratio-related twist, phase, or length-scale ratio). $H_4$ asserts:
1. The topological invariant $\nu$ and the associated transport observable $T$ cannot attain the Baskaboo-predicted values in the absence of $\Phi$, i.e., for any configuration where $\Phi$ is replaced by $\Phi'$ not satisfying a pre-defined relation (e.g., $\Phi' \neq \varphi$, the golden ratio-related parameter).
2. No alternative parameter choice $\Phi'$ yields the same pair $(\nu, T)$ within experimental uncertainty.

* **Failure Mode:** If samples engineered without the special $\Phi$ (or with $\Phi'$) reproduce the same $(\nu, T)$-pair as the purported Baskaboo configuration, $H_4$ is falsified.

## 2. Experimental Methods & Materials

### 2.1 Sample Architecture
We will fabricate three classes of electronic quasicrystalline heterostructures:
* **Monolayer system $S_1$:** Single-layer quasicrystalline electronic structure with defined lattice parameters and controlled disorder.
* **Bilayer system $S_2$:** Two coupled quasicrystal layers forming the hypothesized Double-Pass configuration, tuned to exhibit the specific structural parameter $\Phi$.
* **Trilayer system $S_3$:** Three-layer extension with comparable interlayer coupling, serving as a multi-pass control.

All samples are to be fabricated on identical substrates, under identical growth conditions, and subject to the same post-processing (annealing, encapsulation, contacts), to control for extraneous variability.

### 2.2 Measurement Setup
We will employ low-temperature (dilution refrigerator or equivalent) electronic transport measurements, combined with:
* High-resolution spectroscopy (e.g., ARPES or scanning tunneling spectroscopy) for $\sigma(E)$.
* Phase-sensitive interferometric measurements (Aharonov–Bohm rings, Mach–Zehnder-type setups, or equivalent mesoscopic interferometers) to extract phase-coherence metrics $C_{\text{in}}, C_{\text{out}}$.
* Magneto-transport and non-local measurements to probe topological signatures and directional asymmetries.

All measurements will be performed over statistically sufficient device counts per sample class (pre-registered $N_1, N_2, N_3$) to allow hypothesis testing with a predefined statistical power.

### 2.3 Controlled Bilayer vs. Non-Bilayer Comparison
The core experimental design is a controlled comparison among $S_1, S_2, S_3$:
* Identical measurement protocols across all samples.
* Identical biasing, temperature ranges, and magnetic field sweeps.
* Systematic variation of $\Phi$ (where physically realizable) across a family of bilayer samples, including $\Phi = \Phi^*$ (Baskaboo-prescribed value) and $\Phi' \neq \Phi^*$ (control values).

The analysis will blind the experimentalists to the sample class labels during data acquisition and primary processing to avoid confirmation bias.

## 3. Pre-Registered Endpoints & Metrics

### 3.1 Primary Endpoint: Double-Pass Spectral Signature
Define a bilayer-specific spectral observable $\Delta_{\text{DP}}$ as a function of energy (and optionally external field) derived from $\sigma(E)$. For instance:
$$\Delta_{\text{DP}} = \max_{E \in W} |\sigma_+(E) - \sigma_-(E)|$$
where $\sigma_+, \sigma_-$ denote spectra measured under two opposite or dual transport configurations corresponding operationally to the two “passes”, and $W$ is a pre-selected energy window.

**Pre-registered prediction:**
* **$S_2$:** $\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_2) \geq \Delta_{\text{min}} > 0$ with a specified lower bound $\Delta_{\text{min}}$.
* **$S_1, S_3$:** $\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_1), \Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_3) \leq \epsilon_{\Delta}$, where $\epsilon_{\Delta}$ is a noise-level threshold.

### 3.2 Secondary Endpoint: Topological Invariant–Transport Relation
We pre-register the computation of a K-theoretic invariant $\nu \in K_0(\mathcal{A})$ (e.g., via numerical approximation of a Chern marker, index of a projection, or equivalent) from the effective Hamiltonian extracted or constrained by experiment. We define a transport observable $T$ (quantized conductance plateau, edge-mode count, or robust non-local response) and a fixed map $f$ such that:
$$T_{\text{pred}} = f(\nu)$$

**Prediction:**
* For $S_2$ with $\Phi = \Phi^*$, the measured $T_{\text{exp}}$ satisfies $|T_{\text{exp}} - T_{\text{pred}}| \leq \delta_T$, where $\delta_T$ is a pre-registered tolerance.
* For control geometries ($S_1$, $S_3$, and $S_2$ with $\Phi' \neq \Phi^*$), the Baskaboo-predicted pair $(\nu, T)$ is not realized.

### 3.3 Phase Memory Metric
We define a phase-coherence metric $R$ as:
$$R = \frac{C_{\text{out}}}{C_{\text{in}}}$$
where $C_{\text{in}}$ is extracted from an initialization measurement of interference contrast and $C_{\text{out}}$ after transport through the quasicrystal region under the gapless-flow condition.

We pre-register a functional dependence $R_{\text{pred}} = F(g)$, with $g = 0.00382$, and a tolerance band $\delta_R$. For example, $R_{\text{pred}} = 1 - \alpha g$ with a fixed $\alpha$, or another explicitly specified form.

**Empirical success condition:**
$$|R_{\text{exp}} - R_{\text{pred}}| \leq \delta_R$$
This is evaluated separately for $S_1, S_2, S_3$; only $S_2$ with $\Phi = \Phi^*$ is predicted to satisfy the Baskaboo-specific relation.

## 4. Falsification Criteria & Rejection Rules

To ensure that the Baskaboo framework is genuinely falsifiable, we pre-register the following “kill rules”. If any one of these is met at the pre-defined confidence level, the framework is considered empirically refuted in its present form.

### 4.1 Rejection of $H_2$: Lack of Bilayer Uniqueness
$H_2$ is rejected if any of the following hold:
1. $\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_2) < \Delta_{\text{min}}$ for bilayer samples with $\Phi = \Phi^*$.
2. $\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_1) > \epsilon_{\Delta}$ or $\Delta_{\text{DP}}(S_3) > \epsilon_{\Delta}$ in a manner statistically indistinguishable from $S_2$.
3. The observed bilayer signatures are quantitatively accounted for by standard bilayer models (pre-registered reference models) without invoking the non-commutative structure of $H_{+\Phi}, H_{-\Phi}$.

### 4.2 Rejection of $H_1$ and $H_4$: Invariant–Observable Decoupling and $\Phi$-Forgeability

1. The computed invariant $\nu$ fails to correlate with $T$ according to the pre-specified map $f$, i.e., $|T_{\text{exp}} - f(\nu)| > \delta_T$ across the bilayer samples with $\Phi = \Phi^*$.
2. Control samples with $\Phi' \neq \Phi^*$ (or different structural parameters) reproduce the same $(\nu, T)$ pair, within experimental error, as the purported Baskaboo configuration.

### 4.3 Rejection of $H_3$: Phase-Memory Mismatch
$H_3$ is rejected if:
1. The measured phase-memory ratio $R_{\text{exp}}$ for $S_2$ with $g = 0.00382$ deviates from the pre-registered prediction by more than $\delta_R$: $|R_{\text{exp}} - R_{\text{pred}}| > \delta_R$.
2. Equivalent or superior agreement with $R_{\text{pred}}$ is observed in control systems that do not implement the Baskaboo-prescribed configuration (e.g., $S_1$, $S_3$, or $S_2$ with $\Phi' \neq \Phi^*$).

### 4.4 Global Framework Rejection
We pre-register the following global rejection rule: **If two or more of the hypotheses $H_1$–$H_4$ are rejected under the above criteria, at the agreed significance thresholds, the Baskaboo framework—as a physical theory claiming to underlie quasicrystal-based quantum transport—is considered falsified in its current formulation.**
